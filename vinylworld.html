    • Record player help

    A vinyl record is a flat disc, an analogue sound system that stores sound medium. It is inscribed with a spiral groove. It starts at the periphery and ends nearly in the middle of the disc.

    How to use a record player

    • one has to place it on a turntable platter.
    • An anti-static brush should be used to clean the record after starting the turntable.
    • The tonearm should be raised using a cueing lever and moved across the record.
    • Lower the tonearm after aligning the stylus with where you wish to start playing the record.

    Parts of the Record Player

    Turntable – it is a circular plate where the record is placed. It is covered in plastic or rubber to prevent scratching.

    Stylus – it is not only small but also the most valuable component made of diamond. It has a cone shape. The pointed edge touches the spiral grooves of the disk that picks up vibrations, turning them to sound.

    Tonearm – sits parallel to the record. It is a metallic strip with wires that transit vibrations towards the cartridge.

    Cartridge – turns vibration into electric signals through a coil in the magnetic field.

    Vinyl History

    Thomas Edison was the inventor in 1877. He discovered that sound is a vibration and designed a medium to record the waves so that they could be tuned to sound with a second needle. He then designed a phonograph where tinfoil is wrapped around a brass cylinder that circumscribes when turned by hand. The diaphragm was connected to the needle on the other side. Sound waves entering the receiving end made the membrane vibrate. While the cylinder was being tuned, the needle etched a groove into a foil. This led to a sound being recorded. There was a second needle that acted as an amplifier.

    'Mary had a little lamb' was the first word ever recorded by Thomas Edison through a mouthpiece. Ten years later, Emile Berliner founded Gramophone Company which manufactured the records and machines that played them. However, it was not capable of performing the task of recording and playing back sound on one device. Johnson improved the outlook of the gramophone, which had a big horn to amplify sound. It didn't prove easy to fit in a home. It was later turned downwards and named Victrola.
